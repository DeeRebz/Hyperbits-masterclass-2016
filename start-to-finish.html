FAQ

1. What can I expect to learn from taking this course?

The main goal of this class is to make commercially viable, professional sounding music. You can expect to push creative boundaries, increase your output, create cleaner, more analogue sounding mixes, louder masters with more dynamics, and overall, progress on your artistic journey.

2. How much prior experience do I need to take this class?

If you are an absolute beginner and have never opened a DAW before (or even know what a DAW is), this course is most likely not for you. However, anyone who has spent some time in Logic Pro, Ableton, FL Studio, Bitwig, Reason, or Cubase making music will gain a TON of value. It is recommended you have at least 8-12 months experience before taking this class.

3. Does it matter what Digital Audio Workstation I use?

The content of this course is not DAW specific. Serik will primarily be using Logic X, but most of the ideas discussed in the class will be applicable to any DAW on the market. Also, the course does have an Ableton Masterclass certified expert available to answer any of your Ableton specific questions.

4. What happens if a miss a class?

All classes will be recorded and available for viewing after each session. You can also catch up by asking additional questions during office hours, posting in the Facebook group, or just messaging Serik & Zach on Facebook.

5. When do the live classes and office hours happen?

Students will have the option to select one of two time slots for the live classes: Tuesdays @ 3PM EST or Wednesdays @ 8PM EST. Office hours will vary, but will start Mondays @ 8PM EST & Thursdays @ 5PM EST to accommodate different time zones. Don't forget - all classes are recorded and made available to you in the member site just incase you can't attend.

6. How long do I have access to the recorded videos of the class?

You have lifetime access to ALL recorded content.

7. I am thinking of taking a class elsewhere. Why should I take the Masterclass?

I will never discredit other courses when it comes to music production. Each educational institution provides a different experience that may be a better fit for your style. In fact, I took a mixing and mastering class with Dubspot and man, I learned a lot. That said, I have a unique perspective on the industry because I am an artist first, and a teacher second.

In my opinion, this has allowed me to acquire some fairly unique insights and techniques in the production realm that similar learning institutions simply will not be able to provide. Plus, for all the content I provide, there is no better price in the market.

8. Who is the top-tier, advanced class for?

The entire 8-Week Masterclass is meant for intermediate to advanced producers, but the advanced course is meant for EXTREMELY focused, career driven producers looking for some additional 1 on 1 attention.

This advanced tier is NOT specifically meant for the most technically proficient producers, but rather passionate artists looking for next level guidance, networking, and peer feedback.

9. When do the top-tier classes happen?

Top-tier advanced courses will run Thursday and/or Friday afternoons & evenings (EST) based on availability and scheduling.

10. What plugins & synths will you be primarily using in the class?

Just to be clear - you do NOT need all of the following plugins, but I will be jumping around between: Sylenth, Massive, Refx Nexus 2, Serum, Trilian, as well as mixing plugins like the Sound Toys Bundle, Fab Filter Bundle, Waves Bundle, Izotope Ozone 7, Brainworx Bundle, Sausage Fattner, Nicky Romero's Kickstart, as well as a bunch of free plugins.

11. How often do you offer the Masterclass?

As a one-man team I am doing my best to keep up with demand. My current plan is to run the class 2-3 times per year. The next session will begin March of 2017.

Yes, head over to www.hyperbitsmusic.com to see what services I can provide for your music, as well as my current rates. For further inquiries please get in touch at info AT hyperbitsmusic DOT com.
